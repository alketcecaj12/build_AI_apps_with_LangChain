10 Good Web Page Examples That Nail UX, Design & Copy

By Jaden Montag|Published June 22, 2025|Updated February 3, 2026

In the digital age, where first impressions are often formed online, a website’s design can make or break how users perceive a brand or service. Spotting good web page examples can offer insight and inspiration for those seeking to enhance their sites. This article showcases 10 examples of websites that deliver outstanding user experience (UX), design, and copy, serving as a benchmark for creating engaging and functional web spaces that stand out in 2024’s best website designs. Welcoming you into this journey of creativity and brilliance, let’s delve into page website designs, and amazing website designs that have set new standards.

10 Good Web Page Examples

1. Apple

  • Apple’s site is the epitome of minimalism paired with functionality. Their web page design is simple yet powerful, ensuring users get what they need without unnecessary fluff.
  • The use of white space, clean typography, and high-resolution images allows the product to be the hero while making navigation intuitive.
  • With each scroll, users are pulled into a seamless narrative about cutting-edge technology.
  • Apple's consistent brand image over the years reinforces their authority and innovation, guiding design trends around the globe.

2. Airbnb

  • Airbnb offers a user-friendly interface that balances visuals with text, a clear protocol for a smooth user journey.
  • The homepage’s search functionality is bold and inviting, enabling users to dive directly into searching for properties without delay.
  • Airbnb’s story-telling approach through imagery and customer testimonials exemplifies how copy and visuals can intertwine to engage effectively.
  • Its consistent design language and community-driven content make it trustworthy while promoting a sense of global belonging.

3. Dropbox

  • Dropbox’s webpage drives home its cloud storage benefits with a straightforward approach.
  • The homepage is rich with calls-to-action, enabling easy access to sign-up journeys without overwhelming users.
  • Its crisp illustrations and fun animations help in underscoring the user-friendly nature of its services.
  • The professional layout and straightforward copy reflect Dropbox’s authority in data storage solutions.

4. The New York Times

  • Integrating traditional journalism tenets into a modern layout, the NYT succeeds in having users crave information in a digestible form.
  • A balanced content-to-white-space ratio ensures comfortable reading experiences for users.
  • Unlike generic news sites, it employs striking photography alongside sophisticated design elements to keep users engaged.
  • A longstanding history and analytical depth make it a trusted user resource.

5. TED

  • TED's website channels its insightful talks into an overwhelmingly clean design allowing users to dive into featured talks swiftly.
  • Users can explore various topics via intuitive filter options leading to a personalized browsing voyage.
  • Sharp use of curated embedding of video content facilitates insightful consumption.
  • TED’s collaborative nature brings forth authority in knowledge sharing across fields.

6. Slack

  • Slack’s website reflects easy collaboration, evoking a workplace aesthetic that is relatable and effective.
  • Feature highlights are succinct and neatly organized for effortless navigation.
  • Utilizing playful copy that highlights user engagement subtly improves the perception of workplace efficiency.
  • A familiar and universally accepted interface makes it a staple for modern-day teams.

7. Medium

  • A reader-centered design paying homage to minimalism ensures undistracted reading.
  • Users experience clear categorizations of topics, allowing smooth access to articles of interest.
  • Premium content and immersive design make every read feel valuable.
  • The collaborative platform hosts credible voices from all spheres, adding value to user experiences.

8. Spotify

  • Spotify perfectly captures the musical journey through their sleek and responsive design.
  • Search functionalities and playlists are easily accessible, making navigation seamless.
  • Vivid imagery and engaging recommendations turn it into more than a streaming service.
  • Its well-maintained user interface promotes trust and continual usage.

9. Asana

  • Asana brings project management into a cohesive, visually appealing digital arena.
  • Clean lines and inviting colors lead to a simplistic yet effective design approach.
  • Explanatory animations coupled with informed copy set expectations rightly.Credible: Reinforcement of collaborative efficiency makes it a credible tool for productivity.

10. Marvel

  • Marvel’s site provides a cinematic experience in navigation and overall charisma.
  • A design rich in graphics yet balanced with text, it prompts focused exploration.
  • Immersive storytelling and vivid visuals envelop users into the Marvel universe perpetually.
  • The combination of rich heritage and modern design keeps fans coming back, reinforcing brand loyalty.

FAQ: Good Web Page Examples

How do the top 10 web pages nail UX, design, and copy?

These examples excel in UX, design, and copy by:

  • Focusing on User Needs: Whether it’s the slick navigation of Apple or the personalized experience on Airbnb, each caters to user needs and expectations.
  • Consistent Branding: Each website maintains a clear and consistent brand message and image, reinforcing user trust and brand recognition.
  • Clear Information Architecture: Sites like Medium and Dropbox are structured in a way that users can effortlessly find information.
  • Engaging, Clear Copy: Websites like Slack and Spotify employ direct, compelling language that speaks to their audiences.
  • Visual Hierarchy and Aesthetics: The visual layout of sites like Apple and Trello guide users intuitively, emphasizing critical information through strategic use of color, typography, and spacing.
  • Responsive Design: All these websites ensure functionality and aesthetics across devices, providing a seamless experience on both desktops and mobile devices.

What features do good web pages typically have?

Good web pages generally include:

  • Intuitive Navigation: Easy-to-follow menus and links that help users find what they need quickly.
  • Fast Load Times: Optimized for speedy access to content, improving user retention and satisfaction.
  • Responsive Design: Compatibility across various devices and screen sizes.
  • Accessibility: Features that accommodate various disabilities, ensuring inclusivity.
  • Strong Calls-to-Action (CTAs): Persuasive, action-oriented language encouraging user engagement.
  • Consistent Visuals: Cohesive color schemes, fonts, and branding elements.
  • Engaging Content: Well-written, valuable, and contextually relevant copy.
  • High-Quality Images and Multimedia: Clear, relevant visuals that complement and enhance the content.
  • SEO Best Practices: Optimized for search engines to improve visibility and reach.

Why are these 10 examples considered to have good web design?

The cited examples are considered paragons of good web design because they:

  • Set Industry Standards: These companies are often pioneers, setting benchmarks for web design best practices.
  • Optimize User Journeys: Their design and UX are centered around user tasks and journeys, helping users achieve their goals efficiently.
  • Balance Aesthetics and Functionality: They combine stunning visuals with practical functionality, offering users both form and function.
  • Innovative Use of Technology: Each utilizes cutting-edge technology to enhance user interaction and satisfaction.
  • Data-Driven Design Improvements: They often continuously update and refine their pages based on user data and feedback.
  • High Engagement and Conversion Rates: The effectiveness of these designs is often reflected in user retention and conversion metrics.

By understanding these principles through real-world examples, businesses and designers can strive to create impactful, user-friendly, and memorable web experiences.

Conclusion

Exploring these good web page examples offers invaluable lessons in combining UX, design, and copy to craft digital experiences that are engaging, informative, and memorable. From business titans like Apple and Dropbox, innovative platforms like Airbnb and Slack, to content powerhouses like Medium and TED, these pages exhibit a mastery of integrating interactive elements, clean layouts, and impactful narratives.

Whether you are designing an about page website design or looking to achieve amazing website design status, taking inspiration from these exemplary designs will undoubtedly set a firm foundation for creating a digital presence that resonates with users, reflecting the benchmarks of 2024 best website designs.
